A Warm Welcome!

All of us at some point will have times of feeling overwhelmed by the inevitable uncertainties of life.
The impact this has on our emotional well-being can leave us feeling unsettled or struggling to understand and communicate our feelings and needs to those closest to us.
If you are looking for support with managing difficult feelings, troubling thoughts or problematic relationships, I can help you with finding your way through to a deeper sense of understanding, acceptance and inner connection.
I will listen with empathy and without judgement as we explore together what has brought you here at this time and I will trust in you to find your own answers rather than give advice or offer interpretations.

About Me

- Zoe Barton -

My background was originally in family support work and education which has given me a wealth of experience in working with both adults and young people.
I have worked extensively with people living with common mental health issues such as anxiety and depression alongside managing emotionally challenging life events including family breakdown, illness and bereavement. More intensive family work has also often involved working to support adults that have experienced domestic abuse and addiction.
Previous careers have also given me a unique insight and understanding into the demands and stresses that can often be presented daily to those in professions where they are supporting children and young people impacted by adverse childhood experiences (ACES).
I’m passionate that those working in ‘front-line’ education and health and social care roles with young people have quality access themselves to mental health support. For this reason, I have a limited number of concessionary rate slots available for clients who work in these professions.
My career as a counsellor has additionally included working with adults for the brain injury charity, Headway where I worked with those impacted directly by a brain injury and their family members. Counselling experience with young people has included working in a secondary school with pupils from 11 years old and youth agency work with young people up to the age of 25.
As a counsellor I work in an integrative way which means that I can utilise different ways of working that take account of you as an individual. In my private practice I work both short and long-term supporting adults and older teenagers (16+) presenting with many different issues including:

  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Low Mood
  • Relationship Issues
  • Bereavement
  • Ambiguous Loss
  • Work Related Issues
  • Personal Development
  • Self-esteem
  • Stress
  • Anger

My Qualifications

What will a session together be like?

I have trained to work in an integrative way which means that I can draw on various methods and techniques to take account of your unique needs and goals for therapy. Work together will mainly involve what is sometimes called, ‘talking therapy’ where you will set the agenda with the issues that you wish to bring and pace at which we work. At times, we may look at other ways of working that can enhance the therapeutic experience even further.


Many clients, particularly those experiencing anxiety, can find it beneficial to explore and practise during sessions various forms of coping strategies. We will always discuss and agree on this beforehand and evaluate the effectiveness of different methods in following sessions.

I often employ creative methods of working that can help clients who for many reasons have difficulty ‘finding the right words’ to describe their feelings and emotional responses to difficult life events. This may involve the use of objects, drawing, story or music to stimulate discussion.

I believe in the importance of empowering clients with the use of ‘psychoeducation’, particularly where they may have experienced traumatic life events. Alongside this, I passionately promote the importance of self-care and will talk about you can incorporate this into your life.

Session Arrangements

We will arrange a mutually convenient time to have an initial free telephone call of 20 minutes to discuss in more detail your presenting issues and needs. This will also give an opportunity for you to ask any questions and to get a feel of what working together will be like.
If you decide after our initial call that you would like to start counselling sessions together, we will again arrange a mutually convenient time to meet on a regular basis.
Our therapeutic contract will be client-centred with the aims and goals of therapy being guided by you and your needs which we will review at regular intervals.
Counselling sessions will last for 50 minutes, and it is recommended they take place weekly at the same time.
I am currently offering both face to face sessions from my home in Fleet and online sessions.
