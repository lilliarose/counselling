A Warm Welcome!

All of us at some point will have times of feeling overwhelmed by the inevitable uncertainties of life.
The impact this has on our emotional well-being can leave us feeling unsettled or struggling to understand and communicate our feelings and needs to those closest to us.
If you are looking for support with managing difficult feelings, troubling thoughts or problematic relationships, I can help you with finding your way through to a deeper sense of understanding, acceptance and inner connection.
I will listen with empathy and without judgement as we explore together what has brought you here at this time and I will trust in you to find your own answers rather than give advice or offer interpretations.

About Me

Learn More About Me

- Zoe Barton -

My background was originally in education and family support work which has given me a wealth of experience in working with both young people and adults.
During this time, I worked extensively with people living with common mental health issues such as anxiety and depression alongside managing emotionally challenging life events including family breakdown, illness and bereavement. More intensive family work also often involved working to support those that had experienced domestic violence and addiction.
My work as a counsellor has involved working with young people from the age of 11 in schools and youth agency work up to the age of 25. Adult work has included working with both brain-injured clients and family members impacted by living with and caring for someone with a brain injury through the specialist charity, Headway.
As a counsellor I work in an 'integrative' way which means that I can utilise different ways of working that take account of you as an individual.
In my practice I work both short and long-term supporting young people and adults presenting with many different issues including:

  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Low Mood
  • Relationship Issues
  • Bereavement
  • Ambiguous Loss
  • Work Related Issues
  • Personal Development
  • Self-esteem
  • Stress
  • Anger
